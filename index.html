My story

My Testimony

The Foundation of Self-Reliance

I grew up attending a traditional church in Colorado, but Sunday always felt more like a chore than a relationship. As I got older, I stopped thinking about God entirely. I focused on being a moral, hard-working man.

Becoming a husband and father only reinforced my self-reliance, pride, and self-righteousness. I thought I could handle life on my own terms.

The Turning Point

That changed in September of 2025. My wife Gina and I were navigating a deeply difficult season, heartbroken by the tragic loss of Charlie Kirk. We quickly realized that despite building a happy, productive life, nothing we achieved could satisfy our underlying fear and anxiety.

During this painful time, a faithful friend, Jennifer Clark, shared the gospel with Gina and prayed for her. It resonated deeply.

"If I find in myself a desire which no experience in this world can satisfy, the most probable explanation is that I was made for another world."

— C.S. Lewis

Gripped by the Truth

We began attending church as a family. I dove into the Bible and Christian podcasts, completely gripped by the truth of the Gospel. God showed me three sobering realities:

True Sin

Sin isn't just bad deeds — it's refusing to be who God created me to be.

The Cost

The wages of sin is separation from God, both now and in eternity.

The Rescue

God loves me so much that He came in the flesh, lived a perfect life, and died to pay a debt I couldn't pay.

I came to understand that Satan is perfectly content with "moral men," so long as they live without Jesus Christ and nothing I can do will earn my salvation. Grace through faith alone, in Jesus Christ alone

A New Purpose

Today, I know Jesus is alive, living in me, and seated at the right hand of God. My highest calling is no longer just providing a comfortable life for my family. My true purpose as a husband and father is to lead my family to trust in the finished work of Jesus Christ.

I am incredibly grateful for our church community, and honored to have surrendered my heart to Jesus and been baptized alongside my wife. He has completely transformed my behavior, my perspective, and my priorities.

"Our citizenship is in heaven."

— Philippians 3:20
